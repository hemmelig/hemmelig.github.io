Askja, Iceland

I am currently a post-doctoral research scientist on the AVERT (Anticipating Volcanic Eruptions in Real Time) project at the Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory, Columbia University. The goal of this project is to monitor volcanic unrest using multi-disciplinary instrumentation (GNSS, seismometers, gas emission, webcam), edge computing, and near-real-time data transmission, with an aim to improve our real-time eruption forecasting capabilities.

We are instrumenting Cleveland (Aleut - Chuginadak) and Okmok (Aleut - Unmagim Anatuu) volcanoes, which represent "open" and "closed" volcanic systems—for more information, see the project website.

I completed my PhD at the University of Cambridge, where I worked on microseismic detection and seismic anisotropy in both Iceland and northern Borneo. Fell in love with fieldwork—chasing it ever since.

Free and open science is the future.
